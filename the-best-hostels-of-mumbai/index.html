The Best Hostels of Mumbai

bhavesh July 6, 2024

Exploring Mumbai: The City of Dreams

Mumbai, also known as the “”City of Dreams,”” is a bustling metropolis on the west coast of India. It is a melting pot of cultures and traditions, with a vibrant nightlife, diverse cuisine, and stunning architecture. From iconic landmarks such as the Gateway of India and Marine Drive to the bustling markets of Colaba and Bandra, Mumbai offers a unique blend of old-world charm and modern sophistication. The city is also home to Bollywood, the largest film industry in the world, adding to its allure as a cultural hub. With its rich history and exciting energy, Mumbai is a must-visit destination for budget travelers looking to experience the vibrant spirit of India.

The Best Hostels in Mumbai for Backpackers

GoSolo Stays

Location: Mumbai | 7.07 km away from city center
Why we love it: GoSolo is an all-female hostel that offers a safe and comfortable space for solo women travelers. It’s located in a quiet neighborhood yet close to major attractions, making it a great base for exploring Mumbai. The hostel has a friendly and welcoming atmosphere, with a focus on community and connection.
Price Range: Expect to pay ₹1190 per night on average.
Amenities:

  • Free Wi-Fi
  • Breakfast
  • Common kitchen
  • Rooftop terrace
  • Laundry service
    Rating: 9/10

Indie Stays

Location: Mumbai | 6.03 km away from city center
Why we love it: Indie Stays offers a cozy and welcoming atmosphere, perfect for travelers looking to meet new people and explore the city. The hostel’s focus on community, social events, and unique design make it a favorite among guests.
Price Range: Expect to pay ₹1359.2 per night on average.
Amenities:

  • Free Wi-Fi
  • Common lounge area
  • Shared kitchen facilities
  • Outdoor terrace
    Rating: 9/10

Krazy Solo Hostel

Location: Mumbai | 4.81 km away from city center
Why we love it: This lively and friendly hostel is the perfect place to meet other travelers and experience the local culture. It offers a range of activities and events, including a rooftop terrace, a bar, and a communal kitchen. The staff is friendly and helpful, and they are always willing to go the extra mile to make sure your stay is perfect.
Price Range: Expect to pay ₹999 per night on average.
Amenities:

  • Free Wi-Fi

Travel Tips

  1. Best Time to Travel: The best time to visit Mumbai is between the months of November and February when the weather is cooler and more pleasant. Avoid visiting during the monsoon season (June to September) as heavy rains can disrupt travel plans.
  2. Foods to Try: Mumbai is a paradise for food lovers with a wide variety of street food and local delicacies to try. Don’t miss out on trying Vada Pav, Pav Bhaji, Misal Pav, Pani Puri, and Mumbai’s famous street-side chaat.
  3. Festive Season: One of the best times to visit Mumbai is during the Ganesh Chaturthi festival, which usually takes place in August or September. The city comes alive with colorful processions, cultural events, and local festivities to honor the elephant-headed deity, Lord Ganesha. Don’t miss out on witnessing the grand immersion procession of Ganesh idols in the Arabian Sea.”
