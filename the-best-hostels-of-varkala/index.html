Exploring Varkala: A Budget-Friendly Travel Guide

Image:

Varkala is a coastal town in the southern state of Kerala, India, known for its stunning cliffside views of the Arabian Sea. The town is famous for its serene beaches, ancient temples, and vibrant local markets. Varkala is a popular destination for budget travelers seeking a mix of relaxation and adventure, with opportunities for surfing, yoga, and Ayurvedic treatments. Travel Tips

  1. Best Time to Travel: The best time to visit Varkala is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and cool. Avoid the monsoon season from June to September as the heavy rains can disrupt travel plans.

  2. Foods to Try: When in Varkala, make sure to try the traditional Kerala cuisine, including dishes like appam, fish curry, and banana leaf meals. Don‚Äôt miss out on trying local seafood delicacies like fresh grilled fish and prawn curry.

  3. Festive Season: One of the biggest festivals in Varkala is the Karkidaka Vavubali, which is celebrated in the Malayalam month of Karkidakam (July-August). During this time, prayers and offerings are made to ancestors on the beach. Another popular festival is the Vishu festival, celebrated in April, which marks the Kerala New Year. Enjoy the festive atmosphere and traditional rituals during these times.
