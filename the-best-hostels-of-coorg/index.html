The Best Hostels of Coorg

bhavesh July 6, 2024

Exploring Coorg: A Backpacker’s Paradise

Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is a picturesque hill station located in the state of Karnataka, India. Famous for its lush greenery, coffee plantations, and misty mountains, Coorg offers a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The city is renowned for its cultural and historical attractions, such as the Madikeri Fort, Omkareshwara Temple, and Abbey Falls. Adventurous travelers can also enjoy activities like trekking, river rafting, and bird watching in the scenic Western Ghats. With its pleasant climate and welcoming locals, Coorg is a must-visit destination for budget travelers seeking a peaceful retreat.

The Best Hostels in Coorg for Backpackers

1. The Hosteller Coorg, Suntikoppa

Location: Suntikoppa | 2.82 km away from the city center

Why we love it: Set amidst a tranquil coffee plantation in Suntikoppa, The Hosteller Coorg offers an eco-friendly and cozy retreat. With its focus on sustainability and nature immersion, this hostel is perfect for travelers seeking a rustic and rejuvenating experience.

Price Range: Expect to pay ₹612.47 per night on average.

Rating: Guests typically rate this hostel around 9.4/10.

2. goSTOPS Coorg - Rooms & Dorms

Location: Coorg | 7.11 km away from the city center

Why we love it: This vibrant hostel in the heart of Coorg offers an unforgettable experience for travelers seeking adventure and community. With its stunning views of the Western Ghats, cozy common areas, and lively atmosphere, it’s a haven for those looking to connect, explore, and create lasting memories.

Price Range: Expect to pay ₹1,000 per night on average.

Rating: Guests typically rate this hostel around 9/10.

3. The Hosteller Coorg, Madikeri

Location: Madikeri | 13.43 km away from the city center

Why we love it: The Hosteller Coorg is a vibrant and welcoming hostel nestled amidst the lush greenery of Coorg. With its cozy common areas and spacious rooms, it’s the perfect place to connect with fellow travelers and immerse yourself in the local culture.

Price Range: Expect to pay ₹863.59 per night on average.

Rating: Guests typically rate this hostel around 8.9/10.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to travel: The best time to visit Coorg is during the winter months from October to March when the weather is cool and pleasant.
  • Foods to try: Coorg is known for its delicious cuisine, so be sure to try traditional dishes like pandi curry (pork curry), akki roti (rice pancakes), and Kadambuttu (steamed rice balls).
  • Festive season to look out for: The Kodava people of Coorg celebrate the annual ‘Kailpodh’ festival in July and the ‘Puttari’ festival in November, both of which are colorful and vibrant events that showcase the rich cultural heritage of the region.
